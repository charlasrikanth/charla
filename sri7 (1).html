This website shows different forms of wildlife.

LION

The lion is a species in the family Felidae, and a member of the genus Panthera. It exhibits a pronounced sexual dimorphism; males are larger than females with a typical weight range of 150 to 250 kg.


TIGER

The tiger is the largest cat species, most recognizable for its pattern of dark vertical stripes on reddish-orange fur with a lighter underside. The species is classified in the genus Panther.


LEOPARD

The leopard is one of the five species in the genus Panthera, a member of the Felidae.The leopard occurs in a wide range in sub-Saharan Africa and parts of Asia and is listed as Vulnerable on the Red List.


HYENA

Hyenas or hyaenas are animals of the family Hyaen. With only four extant species, it is the fifth-smallest biological family.They are good predators.They live and hunt in groups.


WILDEBEEST

The wildebeests, also called gnus, are a genus of antelopes, scientific name Connochaetes. They belong to the family Bovidae.


RHINO

Rhinoceros have a herbivorous diet, small brains for mammals of their size, one or two horns, and a protective skin formed from a lattice structure.


ANTELOPE

An antelope is a member of even-toed ungulate species indigenous in Africa and Eurasia. Antelopes comprise within the family Bovidae.


PANDA

The giant panda is a bear of south China.It is easily found by the large, distinctive black patches around its eyes and across its round body.
